This is something I've been noticing inside myself lately. At this time and age  when we have massive LLM-generated texts, I kind of start to see the beauty in raw writing with a few mistakes here and there. Or in imperfectly written sentences.

As a non-native English speaker, it's very tempting to use LLMs to generate perfect, flawless sentences in a language that's not my mother tongue. And I don't see anything wrong in that because they really are just tools that can make life easier.

But the experience becomes a lot more different when I try to formulate my thoughts in English by myself. Something that's not easy to even accomplish in my mother language. Surely there will be a lot of mistakes here and there. But what matters is I'm taking the time and effort to actually write. It challenges me and it's what makes it enjoyable.
